Single European Act

1986
  • First amending treaty.
  • A ‘step towards European union’.
  • Introduced co-operation procedure → enhanced role for European Parliament.
  • Created the Court of First Instance (now General Court) → assists the CJ.
  • Increased use of Qualified Majority Vote (QMV) in Council → overcomes difficulties with unanimity.
  • New competences.
  • Legal basis for monetary union.

Treaty of European Union

1992
  • Aimed to create a European Monetary Union.
  • Established the ‘pagoda structure’ with three pillars.
  • Pillar 1: European Community Common Policies, N.B. citizenship, subsidiarity, human rights. EEC → EC.
  • Pillar 2: Common Foreign and Security Policy: external action, peace and security.
  • Pillar 3: Police and Judicial Cooperation in Criminal Matters: develop cooperation and coherence amongst European police forces in certain areas.

Treaty of Amsterdam

1997
  • ‘Housekeeping Treaty’.
  • Prepare the institutions for further enlargement.
  • Retained the Pillar structure.
  • Created position of High Representative for Common Foreign and Security Policy (CFSP).
  • Co-decision extended to more areas of Union competence, further strengthening the role of the EP in law-making by rendering it colegislator with the Council.
  • More areas brought under QMV in Council.
  • Renumbered Treaty of Rome articles and TEU 1992 articles fromletters to numbers.
  • Enhanced co-operation.
  • Petersberg Tasks.

Treaty of Nice

2001
  • ‘Leftovers Treaty’.
  • 'to complete the process started by the Treaty of Amsterdam in preparing for enlargement’ (institutional reform).
  • Amendments under Pillar 1.
  • Amendments to CFSP.
  • Amendments to Policing and Joint Co-operation in Criminal Matters.
  • Treaty rejected in Irish referendum, led to significant amendments re Commission size, guarantees on neutrality.

Constitutional Treaty

2004
  • European Council Laeken Summit 2001: Calls for re-visiting ofthe treaties.
  • ‘Convention on the Future of Europe’.
  • June 2003: Convention proposed a Treaty establishing a Constitution for Europe to replace the existing EU and EC treaties.
  • Following an intergovernmental conference (IGC) in 2004, the Constitutional Treaty was to be ratified by the MSs.
  • Not passed by referendum in France, Netherlands.
  • ‘Period of reflection’ before formal abandonment at Brussels summit in June 2007.
