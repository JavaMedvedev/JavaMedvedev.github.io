A husband and two daughters went to the airport to collect the plaintiff’s niece. Some time later the niece telephoned the plaintiff and informed her that her husband and daughters had been seriously injured in a road accident. The plaintiff, it is stated, went into shock and was vomiting and became quite upset. She became further traumatized on visiting the hospital. Ultimately the husband and one of the daughters suffered brain damage, and the other daughter made a full recovery.

Was the PTSD as a result of shock? Defendants argued that the PTSD came about as a result of grief and not shock. This was rejected by the court.
Basis for recovery?: Ultimately Lavan J adopted the reasoning of Denham J in Mullally (ie that of foreseeability) in finding that the defendant was liable.
