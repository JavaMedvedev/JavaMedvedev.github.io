Victoria Ry Commrs v Coultas (1888) 13 AC 222:
A railway guard waved Mr and Mrs Coultas through a railway crossing having failed to see an oncoming train coming down the tracks. They got through without physical injury but Mrs Coultas fainted and suffered what was described as mental shock.

Despite the negligence of the railway guard no liability was imposed as it was stated that the injury was unforeseeable. As stated in that case: Damages arising from mere sudden terror unaccompanied by any actual physical injury, but occasioning a nervous or mental shock, cannot under such circumstances, their Lordships think, be considered a consequence which, in the ordinary course of things, would flow from the negligence of the gate-keeper.
